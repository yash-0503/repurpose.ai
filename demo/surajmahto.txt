Now I will give a brief introduction about SSB.

SSB is a five-day interview process. It tests three things: psychology, interview, and ground testing.

On the first day, there is a screening process which includes OIR test and PPDT. Those who clear screening go to stage two. Others return the same day.

In stage two, there are psychology tests conducted by psychologists. Then there is a personal interview conducted by a senior officer. They try to assess your OLQs (Officer Like Qualities).

After that, there is GTO (Ground Testing Officer) tasks like PGT, HGT, IO tasks, etc.

On the last day, there is a conference where all assessors sit together and decide whether the candidate should be recommended or not.

After recommendation, there is documentation and medical examination. In my case, medicals were conducted at AFCME Delhi.

Initially, I got two temporary rejections. One was for overweight and the second was due to LASIK surgery. After re-medical and appeal medical board, finally I was declared fit.

Then comes the merit list. Based on order of merit, candidates are selected to join the Air Force Academy in Hyderabad.

The entire process takes almost one year, so patience is very important.

Now talking about AFCAT exam.

AFCAT is a computer-based test. The syllabus includes English, Reasoning, Maths, and General Knowledge.

There is no sectional cutoff. Total marks are 300. There is negative marking, so attempt questions carefully.

Generally, the cutoff is around 130–150 marks.

For preparation, I did not join coaching. I prepared from YouTube and online resources. Everything is available online.

The most important thing is revision. The more you revise, the better your performance.

Time management is very important, especially if you are working a full-time job. I used to wake up early, utilize travel time, and study late at night.

Now talking about business analyst roles.

Specifically in product development, stakeholder management is very important. You need to work with quality, development, and project management teams.

A business analyst needs to understand the product, requirements, prioritization, and resource management.

Knowledge of tools like JIRA, Confluence, and documentation is very helpful. These skills help a lot during interviews.

Now talking about exam strategy.

First, scan the paper and solve easy questions. In the second round, solve moderate questions. In the third round, attempt only those where you are 75% sure.

Approximation techniques help a lot in maths. Concept clarity is very important.
